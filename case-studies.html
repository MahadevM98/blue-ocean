Case studies

Quiet exits from crowded markets.

Client names are withheld by agreement. What follows is how each one left a red ocean — and what the open water was worth.

B2B SaaS

From feature war to category of one

A mid-sized platform was matching rivals feature-for-feature and competing on price. Game-theoretic mapping showed every feature race ended in a margin-destroying tie. We repositioned them around a workflow no competitor was structured to serve.

Result: price competition removed from 70% of new deals.
Consumer finance

Designing for the decision, not the demo

Acquisition costs were climbing across an entire category. Behavioural modelling revealed the real barrier was a single moment of perceived risk. We redesigned the onboarding sequence around that bias rather than the demographic.

Result: activation up, blunt-instrument ad spend down.
Health & wellness

Holding the position before naming it

A challenger brand faced three larger incumbents. Using a superposition approach, we kept several positions open while testing, then collapsed into the one the market rewarded — before competitors could read the signal.

Result: defensible niche claimed ahead of the field.
Industrial tech

Out-thinking a deeper-pocketed rival

A competitor could outspend them ten to one. We modelled the rival's incentives and found a segment they were structurally unwilling to chase — then built the whole strategy there.

Result: growth without entering a spending war.
